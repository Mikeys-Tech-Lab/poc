Sensible Defaults is a delivery realism lens. It makes visible the work that planning often ignores.

The full lens text lives in `docs/practices/sensible-defaults.md`. This page summarizes how to use it.

## When to use

Use Sensible Defaults before committing to scope, sequencing, or dates. It forces a minimal delivery trace.

## Minimal trace questions

- What outcome is being created, and how will it be checked?
- Which delivery domains are active, and which are unaccounted for?
- What is the next check that will reduce uncertainty early?

## Nine work domains

All initiatives implicitly require activity across these domains. They are delivery forces, not job titles:

1. **Business analysis**: problem framing, outcome clarification, requirement negotiation
2. **Data and AI**: ethical data usage, bias validation, monitoring, capacity planning
3. **Experience design**: usability, accessibility, research cycles, prototype testing
4. **Infrastructure**: automation, environment reliability, pipeline creation, deployment safety
5. **Product**: outcome definition, direction updates, opportunity discovery, success checks
6. **Coordination**: planning, transparency, shared-understanding conversations
7. **Quality analysis**: early validation, risk discovery, exploratory testing
8. **Security**: risk reduction, access control, threat modeling, vulnerability remediation
9. **Software development**: sustainable implementation, CI/CD, test design, refactoring

## Output shape (when invoked)

1. **Active domains**: domains explicitly covered
2. **Missing domains**: domains likely required but not planned (Routine / Elevated / Critical)
3. **Often unplanned work**: concrete activities that will still happen
4. **Risk concentrations**: where reality will punish optimism
5. **Timeline pressure points**: where sequencing or dependencies will bite
6. **Suggested next questions**: what needs to be clarified before estimating

## Misuse guard

This lens is being misused if it becomes compliance scoring, surveillance, performance evaluation, exhaustive documentation for low-stakes work, a blame tool, or a way to convert uncertainty into narrative instead of a next check.

If it adds pressure instead of releasing it, it has drifted.
