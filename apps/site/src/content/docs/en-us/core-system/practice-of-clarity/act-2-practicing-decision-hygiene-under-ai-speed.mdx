Modern systems can now generate plausible answers faster than teams can fully examine them.

This condition is not new.

High delivery pressure has always pushed teams to move faster than they can fully integrate what they are doing.

AI does not create this condition.

It amplifies it by increasing the volume of plausible output that must be understood, questioned, and integrated.

This creates a shift.

The constraint is no longer access to options.  
The constraint is the ability to work with those options without losing understanding.

Act I described the condition.

Act II begins with a different question:

What changes in practice when speed is no longer the limiting factor?

### The discipline

When output accelerates, teams cannot rely on implicit understanding.

What was previously carried informally now has to become visible.

Decision hygiene is the practice of keeping reasoning in view while work is still moving.

Without this, teams compensate in predictable ways.

Assumptions are left implicit to maintain speed.  
Questions are postponed because there is no time.  
Agreement is taken as a signal of understanding.

Work continues to move.

Understanding does not keep up.

Not as documentation after the fact.  
Not as justification once a decision is made.

But as part of the decision itself.

This changes the unit of work.

The unit is no longer just the artifact:

- a document
- a design
- a piece of code

The unit becomes:

- the artifact
- the assumptions behind it
- the uncertainties that remain

In practice, this can show up in a pull request, a design note, or a planning conversation where reasoning is kept visible alongside the work.

  <p>A generated implementation appears correct.</p>

    It compiles. <br />

  </ul>
  <p>The issue is not that the code is wrong.</p>
  <p>The issue is that the reasoning behind it is absent.</p>
  <p>Drift begins at the point where plausibility replaces inspection.</p>

  <p>A plan is produced quickly.</p>
  <p>
    Everyone reviews it. <br />

    The work begins.

  <p>Weeks later, inconsistencies appear.</p>
  <p>Each part of the system reflects a slightly different interpretation of the same plan.</p>
  <ReadingBeat>Agreement was visible. Understanding was not.</ReadingBeat>
  <p>The plan functioned as a shared artifact, but not as shared reasoning.</p>

  <p>But the unresolved questions do not disappear.</p>

  <p>Speed was maintained. <br />Clarity was deferred.</p>

### What this discipline does

Decision hygiene does not slow work down in the way teams often fear.

It makes visible where speed is introducing hidden risk.

It changes where effort is applied.

Instead of accelerating output alone, it stabilizes how decisions are made under that speed.

It introduces small, continuous checks:

- What are we assuming here?
- What is still unclear?
- Where could this drift?

These are not formal gates or checkpoints.

They are part of how work moves.

### Misuse

<ReadingFrame
  variant="boundary"
  label="Boundary statement"
  title="This is not another process layer"
>
  <p>This practice is often mistaken for:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>more documentation</li>
    <li>heavier process</li>
    <li>additional approval layers</li>
  </ul>
  <p>It is none of these.</p>
  <p>If it becomes overhead, it has already failed.</p>
  <p>A common failure mode is turning this into a requirement or metric.</p>
  <ReadingBeat>
    If teams are asked to "show their reasoning" as a deliverable, it becomes
    performance.
  </ReadingBeat>
  <p>The practice is not about producing evidence.</p>
  <p>This creates a tension that cannot be resolved by policy.</p>
  <p>
    Reasoning must be visible enough to support understanding, but not
    formalized in a way that turns it into performance.
  </p>
  <p>
    If it becomes something to complete, measure, or report on, teams will
    optimize for appearance rather than clarity.
  </p>
  <p>This discipline cannot be enforced from the outside.</p>
  <p>It has to be practiced in the flow of work itself.</p>
  <p>It is about keeping understanding alive while decisions are being made.</p>
  <p>The purpose is not to record everything.</p>
  <p>The purpose is to make the right things visible at the right moment.</p>
</ReadingFrame>

### Forward edge

Making reasoning visible at the level of individual decisions is only the first step.

As systems grow, reasoning does not stay contained.

It moves.

It diverges.  
It reconnects.  
It drifts across boundaries.

To work with this, we need a way to understand how reasoning travels through a system.
