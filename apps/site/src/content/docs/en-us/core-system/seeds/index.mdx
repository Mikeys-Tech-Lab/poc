Seeds are reasoning-contract texts that help the practice keep its shape as it
moves.

They hold commitments the public pages cannot carry all at once: how reasoning
is made visible, how uncertainty stays present, how boundaries are kept, and how
trace remains available under pressure.

People can read them to understand what the practice requires. AI systems can
load them as part of a source context, so responses are shaped by the same
constraints.

Return here when you want the grounding behind the public pages, signals,
lenses, and inspection surfaces.
