This seed is the root reasoning contract for the Practice of Clarity.

It names the core shift from output toward trace: when answers arrive faster
than shared understanding, the reasoning path behind a claim has to remain
visible enough to inspect.

It also sets an important boundary. This is a living practice, not a protocol,
software standard, product, or belief system. Today, the public site is the
primary human-facing surface and the repository is the inspectable operational
node behind it. Federation describes what this practice could become if others
adopt it. It is not a claim that a multi-node community exists now.

### What it helps clarify

- why visible reasoning matters more than polished output
- why clarity is a practice, not a style or belief system
- why AI output must be treated as draft unless its claims are independently checked
- why assumptions, uncertainty, sources, context, and differences need to stay visible
- why summaries that lose trace also lose clarity
- why the public site is the primary reading surface and the repository is the inspectable operational node behind it
- why federation is a future-facing shape, not an active-system claim

### When to read it

Read this when you want the source contract behind the whole practice: trace
over assertion, visible assumptions, declared uncertainty, human responsibility
when AI assists, and the boundary between one inspectable public node and
possible future federation.
