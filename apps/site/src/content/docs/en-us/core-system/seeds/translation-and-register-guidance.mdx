This seed holds the reasoning contract for moving the practice across language,
locale, register, and context without quietly changing meaning.

It matters when the same work needs to become readable in another voice or
language while keeping its claims, boundaries, safety constraints, and reasoning
path accountable.

### What it helps clarify

- the difference between language, locale, register, and adaptation
- why translations are accountable editions, not secondary copies
- what can change as the work moves, and what must stay stable
- why register changes must not change the core claim or safety boundary
- how adaptation can stay honest instead of becoming silent drift
- why cultural resonance matters more than word-for-word conversion

### When to read it

Read this when you want the source contract behind translation, register shifts,
and cultural adaptation: what may change, what must stay stable, and how drift
or co-option should be marked when the work moves.
