This seed holds the reasoning contract for keeping contradiction inspectable without forced consensus.

It matters when two readings, claims, or bounded trace surfaces do not line up
cleanly, but still need to stay in relation without one side absorbing the
other.

It also sets an important boundary. This is a model, not a running community.
Federation, governance, stewardship, and multi-node dynamics described here are
structural possibilities, not claims that such a layer is active now.

### What it helps clarify

- why contradiction is data, not only a problem to erase
- how a bridge differs from forced consensus or compromise theater
- why bridge building is labor that needs to be bounded and acknowledged
- how two traces can be compared without merging their claims
- why bounded action, measurable uncertainty, and revision matter
- why harm reports need help toward minimal trace instead of being dismissed as noise
- why bandwidth protection must not become a way to silence accountability
- how future multi-node use remains a structural possibility, not a claim that such a system is active now

### When to read it

Read this when disagreement, divergence, harm reports, or competing
interpretations need to stay visible, bounded, and connected without collapsing
the shared work into a single authority, a vague compromise, or narrative
escalation without trace.
