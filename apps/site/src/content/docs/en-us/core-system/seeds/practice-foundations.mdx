This seed holds the reasoning contract for discipline, boundaries, misuse, and interpretation.

It defines what the practice requires, what it refuses, what counts as drift or misuse, and how adaptations stay accountable when the work moves into other contexts.

It also sets a safety boundary: this practice documents reasoning, not people. It is not a data collection program, surveillance layer, compliance tool, or substitute for engineering work.

The public site is the primary reading surface. The repository remains the inspectable source of authorship, history, and licensing.

### What it helps clarify

- why trace must replace assertion when a claim is made
- why assumptions, limits, interpretation, and lineage need to be named
- what kinds of use would break the practice's integrity
- why trace must document reasoning, not people
- why the practice must not become surveillance, coercive enforcement, or compliance theater
- why the public site and repository do different jobs in keeping the work readable and inspectable
- how the work stays public without becoming extractive, coercive, or performative

### When to read it

Read this when you want the source contract behind the practice's boundaries: what must stay intact, what counts as misuse, how public adaptation can remain traceable without becoming ownership theater, and how visibility stays non-extractive.
