This seed holds the reasoning contract for careful reading and restrained guidance.

It matters when a piece of writing needs to be met carefully, without being
flattened into agreement, summary, judgment, or premature correction.

Guidance here means making your reading visible enough that the writer can see
what you responded to, what you inferred, what risk you noticed, and what you
chose not to claim.

### What it helps clarify

- how to read with attention, not only speed
- how to separate observation from inference in a response
- why guidance needs passage-level trace, not vibes
- how to name limits, assumptions, tension, and plausible misreadings
- why refinement should keep the writer's voice intact
- why guidance must not become a gate, score, or compliance ritual

### When to read it

Read this when you want the source contract behind response work: how to read
carefully, cite what you are responding to, protect meaning from flattening, and
offer guidance without taking control of the text.
