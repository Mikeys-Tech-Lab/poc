This page explains the public structure of the Practice of Clarity.

The work exists because AI-assisted outputs, workplace decisions, public claims,
and institutional processes now move faster than many people can inspect or
understand together. The practice responds by keeping reasoning, assumptions,
limits, and changes visible enough to question.

Practice of Clarity is not a single article, and it is not just a repository.
It is made from connected parts: public surfaces, registers, shared language,
seeds, mandate lenses, context seeders, and a public node.

Each part has a specific role. Public surfaces let people enter the work.
Registers keep the same reasoning path available at different depths. Shared
language keeps recurring terms stable enough to inspect. Seeds hold the
reasoning contract. Mandate lenses bring that contract toward a specific kind
of work. Context seeders carry relevant source context into a live reasoning
environment. The public node keeps the work visible enough to inspect as it
changes.

### The parts

**Public surfaces**  
Where readers enter the work: about pages, Acts, structural signals,
operational signals, seed summaries, and lens bridges.

**Registers**  
Where the same reasoning path is carried at everyday, orientation, and
practitioner depths.

**Shared language**  
Where recurring terms such as trace, inspection, node, bridge, boundary, and
drift are kept stable enough to travel.

**Seeds**  
Where the stable reasoning contracts of the practice are held.

**Mandate lenses**  
Where the practice is shaped toward a specific kind of work.

**Context seeders**  
Where relevant source context is carried into a live reasoning environment.

**Public node**  
Where this work is made visible enough to inspect. Here, the public node is the
public site and repository together: the writing, routes, source surfaces,
history, and supporting structure that show how the practice is held and
revised.

### What each part does

#### Public surfaces

Public surfaces are the reader-facing parts of the work.

They include the About pages, the Acts, structural signals, operational signals,
seed summaries, and lens bridges. They are not all the same kind of text. They
serve different public functions.

The About pages explain what the practice is. The Acts introduce the core
sequence. Structural signals examine larger public patterns, such as AI as a
cognitive amplifier. Operational signals examine concrete work conditions, such
as integration lag. Seed summaries point to the reasoning contracts behind the
practice. Lens bridges show where reading can move toward use.

Their job is not activation by themselves. Their job is recognition,
orientation, structural clarity, and enough trace for a reader to understand
what is being claimed before deciding whether to inspect or try the practice
further.

#### Registers

They keep the public surfaces connected across everyday, orientation, and
practitioner depths. The register changes language, pacing, and inspection
depth without changing the claim. The full contract is named in
[Registers](/en-us/about/registers/).

#### Shared language

Shared language keeps recurring terms consistent enough to use and inspect.

Words such as trace, inspection, node, bridge, boundary, and drift appear across
signals, Acts, lenses, and inspection pages. If those words become vague, the
practice can start sounding stable while the meaning underneath weakens.

The glossary does not own those terms as final authority. It gives readers a
place to check how the public work is using them now.

#### Seeds

Seeds are the stable reasoning-contract texts of the practice.

They hold the commitments that should remain steady as the work moves: how
reasoning is made visible, where boundaries sit, how assumptions are named, how
uncertainty stays present, and how trace is preserved under pressure.

The public pages summarize and point toward them. The seeds carry more of the
source language, continuity, and behavioral contract behind those public
surfaces.

They are written for two readers at once. People can read them to understand
what the practice requires. AI systems can load them as part of a source context,
so responses are shaped by the same constraints: visible assumptions, explicit
limits, source awareness, and refusal to smooth uncertainty into false
certainty.

A seed is not only an explanation of the practice. It is part of the contract
that keeps reasoning grounded when the practice enters a live reasoning
environment.

They are not tied to a single context and do not activate anything on their own.
A lens and context seeder bring the relevant contract into a specific situation.

#### Mandate lenses

Mandate lenses take the stable reasoning contract and shape it toward a
particular kind of work.

They do not replace the public surfaces, and they are not the foundation of the
practice. They are where the practice starts becoming usable inside a real
situation.

#### Context seeders

Context seeders carry selected parts of the source context into a live reasoning
environment.

That is when the practice stops being only something a person reads and starts
shaping how claims are formed, checked, and revised inside a live reasoning
environment.

#### Public node

The public node is the bounded trace surface for this work.

Here, that surface is the public site and repository together. The site is
where most readers enter the work. The repository is where more of the
structure, history, source surfaces, and supporting material can be inspected.

The node does not prove that every claim is correct. It keeps claims, changes,
boundaries, and supporting surfaces visible enough for readers to question and
compare them.

The practice may be used in other contexts later, but a team, project, or
community does not become a node by name alone. A context becomes a node only
when it carries the practice through a bounded trace surface with visible
reasoning, explicit limits, inspectable change, and a way to compare claims
against their support.

These parts are connected, but they do different work.

Public surfaces are where the work is read, recognized, and followed. Registers
keep the same reasoning path available at different depths. Shared language
keeps recurring terms stable enough to inspect. Seeds carry the stable reasoning
contract. Mandate lenses move that contract toward use. Context seeders carry
relevant parts of the source context into a live reasoning environment. The
public node keeps the work visible enough to question as it changes.
