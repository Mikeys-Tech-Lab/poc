This map shows how the main parts of the practice relate without collapsing
them into one thing.

Each part matters because public reasoning needs more than finished writing. It
needs entry points, stable language, ways to move into use, and inspectable
surfaces where change can be followed.

### The parts

**Public writing**  
Where the condition and the practice can be entered.

**Registers**
Where the same reasoning path is entered at different depths.

**Seeds**  
Where the core texts of the practice are held in stable form.

**Mandate lenses**  
Where the practice becomes specific to a kind of work.

**Context seeders**  
Where a lens is brought into a working environment.

**Public node**  
Where the work can be inspected, evolved further, and made visible over time.  
One example is the [repository](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc). A
team, project, or community can host a node, but the node is the bounded trace
surface, not the people by default.

### What each part does

#### Public writing

It is where the condition, the language, and the practice can be encountered without requiring anything from the reader yet. Its job is not activation. Its job is orientation, recognition, and enough clarity for the work to become legible.

#### Registers

They keep the public writing connected across everyday, orientation, and
practitioner depths. The register changes language, pacing, and inspection
depth without changing the claim. The full contract is named in
[Registers](/en-us/about/registers/).

#### Seeds

They hold the core texts that keep the practice stable.

They hold how reasoning is made visible, where boundaries sit, and how the work holds together under pressure.

They are written so they can be read by people and loaded into reasoning environments.  
They shape how thinking and responses stay grounded.

They are not tied to a single context and do not activate anything on their own.

#### Mandate lenses

They take what is stable in the practice and bring it toward a particular kind of work. They do not replace the public writing, and they are not the foundation. They are where the practice starts becoming usable inside a real situation.

#### Context seeders

They bring selected parts of the work into a live reasoning environment. That
is when the practice stops being only something you read and starts shaping how
claims are formed, checked, and revised in real time.

#### Public node

It holds the materials and the visible history of how the work changes over time. It is where the architecture can be traced more fully, not because the repository is the practice, but because it makes the evolution of the work visible and inspectable.

The repository is one place where this work is made visible. It is not the only
place it can exist.

These parts are connected, but they do different work.

Public writing is for reading. Registers keep entry and inspection connected.
Mandate lenses are for application. Context seeders are for activation. The
public node is for inspection, evolution, and trace.
