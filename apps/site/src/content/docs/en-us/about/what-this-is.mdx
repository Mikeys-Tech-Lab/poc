The pressure is not only volume.

It is speed without integration. Output arrives before people have had time to absorb what it means.

Practice of Clarity is a response to that condition. It keeps reasoning visible while work is still moving, so assumptions, uncertainty, and responsibility do not disappear behind speed.

### What this is

This is a practice, not a product or a protocol.

It is a discipline for keeping judgment inspectable instead of letting decisions vanish into acceleration.

It asks people and machine-assisted workflows alike not to hide the path from observation to inference.

### What it changes

The goal is not better-looking answers.

The goal is to make the reasoning around real work visible enough to question, revise, and share.

That means:

- naming assumptions instead of hiding them
- separating observation from inference
- keeping uncertainty speakable
- leaving a trace that can be inspected and revised later

### What this is not

This is not:

- surveillance
- forced self-documentation
- performative productivity
- pressure to sound certain when you are not
- one-way transparency that exposes people without protection

If making reasoning visible becomes another way to extract labor or expose people downward, it has left the practice.

### Reading and operating are different

This page explains the practice.

It does not place you inside it.

Reading can orient you. It cannot load the posture the practice depends on.

That shift begins only when the first mandate lens is actually loaded through its context seeder.
