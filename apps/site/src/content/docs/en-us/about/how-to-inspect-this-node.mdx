Inspection begins with the claim, not with trust in the presentation.

The Practice of Clarity asks a claim to carry enough of its path to be checked:
what it rests on, what it assumes, what remains uncertain, what changed, and
where a reader can find the boundary. Here, the public site and repository
together form one public node where that path is made visible.

The minimum threshold is qualitative but real: visible reasoning, explicit
limits, inspectable change, and some surface where a claim can be compared with
what supports it.

The repository matters because it gives the public work a deeper trace surface.
It is not proof by itself. A visible file tree, public history, or rendered page
can still be incomplete, mistaken, or read too quickly. Inspection means
comparing surfaces and asking what each one can actually show.

Inspection is not a demand that every reader do the same amount of research.
The register system keeps entry and inspection connected: everyday lets someone
begin from recognition, orientation names more of the structure, and practitioner
keeps the fuller inspection path available.

### What a public node can show

A public node can show the public surfaces, reasoning contracts, lenses,
activation surfaces, route structure, and visible history that hold the work
together.

That matters because clarity is not only a finished statement. It is also the
discipline of keeping the statement inspectable after it moves.

In this node, a reader can see:

- the public surfaces that explain, signal, and carry the practice
- the seeds that hold the stable reasoning contracts
- the mandate lenses that shape those contracts toward specific work
- the context seeders that carry relevant source context into a live reasoning environment
- the route, register, and licensing surfaces that keep public entry coherent
- the history through which the work has been revised

Those surfaces do not all have the same authority. Public surfaces orient the
reader. Seeds hold the reasoning contract. Mandate lenses shape the contract
toward use. Context seeders carry relevant source context into a specific
reasoning situation. History shows movement over time.

Inspection works by respecting those differences.

### What to inspect first

Start with the claim the text is making.

Then ask what surface would let that claim be checked.

If the claim is about the public practice, the relevant surface is usually the
public page and its surrounding section. If the claim is about the reasoning
contract, the relevant surface is usually a seed. If the claim is about trying a
lens, the relevant surface is the lens and source context behind it. If the
claim is about public navigation, the relevant surface is the route map,
sidebar, and content files that make the route reachable.

Selected inspection hooks in the public repository include:

- [`apps/site/src/content/`](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc/tree/main/apps/site/src/content), for the public content surfaces
- [`apps/site/src/lib/route-map.js`](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc/blob/main/apps/site/src/lib/route-map.js), for the public route and register map
- [`seeds/`](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc/tree/main/seeds), for the reasoning-contract texts that stabilize the practice
- [`mandateLenses/`](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc/tree/main/mandateLenses), for lenses and context seeders that carry the practice toward use through source context
- [`docs/architecture/workspace.md`](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc/blob/main/docs/architecture/workspace.md), for deeper repository architecture detail
- [`AGENTS.md`](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc/blob/main/AGENTS.md), for repository-local AI agent behavior and workspace discipline

The public repository is available at [github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc).

These hooks are starting points for inspection. They are not a demand that every
reader become a maintainer, and they are not a shortcut around judgment.

### How inspection stays honest

Inspection becomes dishonest when the existence of a surface is treated as the
same thing as verification.

A repository can show that a piece of public writing exists. It cannot, by
itself, prove that the writing is wise, complete, or correctly understood.

A history can show that something changed. It cannot, by itself, prove that the change was enough.

A guidance file can show an intended discipline. It cannot, by itself, prove that every future action followed it.

The honest move is narrower:

1. name the claim
2. name the surface that can bear on it
3. compare the claim with that surface
4. mark what remains uncertain
5. decide whether the remaining uncertainty matters for the reader's use

That is inspection in the practice's sense. It keeps trace visible without pretending trace has ended the need for judgment.

### What not to expect

This is not a developer guide.

It does not ask the reader to operate the repository, reproduce the site
locally, or turn inspection into a technical ritual. The public node is
available because some readers will want a deeper trace, and because public
practice should leave enough surface for challenge.

Most readers can stay with the public surfaces. The repository is there when the
question becomes structural: Where is this claim held? What governs this
behavior? What changed? What boundary is declared?

  The repository is a trace surface. The practice is still tested by whether its
  claims can be followed, questioned, revised, and used without losing their
  boundaries.
</ReadingBeat>

### A bounded way to read the node

Read from the public claim back toward its support.

Do not start by treating every file as equally important. That creates noise, not clarity.

The useful path is usually:

1. read the piece of writing that made the claim
2. identify the term, boundary, or structural promise you want to inspect
3. look for the surface that is supposed to hold that promise
4. compare what the surface says with what the writing implied
5. keep any mismatch visible

When there is a mismatch, the practice does not require a quick defense. It requires a trace: what differs, what may explain the difference, what would need to change, and what should not be claimed until it is checked.
