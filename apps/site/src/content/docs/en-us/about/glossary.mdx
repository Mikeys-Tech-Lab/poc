This glossary keeps recurring terms short and findable.

It holds two kinds of language:

- the main parts that shape the work
- the smaller words that keep returning while you read or think with it

Definitions are minimal by design. They protect meaning without turning the
glossary into another essay.

---

### Seeds

Seeds are the core texts that hold this work together.

They hold how reasoning is made visible, where boundaries sit, and how the practice stays intact under pressure.

They are written to be read by people and loaded into AI systems.  
They shape how both think and respond.

They keep the practice structurally grounded as it moves.

### Mandate lenses

Mandate lenses are ways the practice turns toward a specific kind of work.

They bring what stays stable in the seeds into a real context.
They are expected to evolve through use and feedback.

They are not the foundation of the practice.
They are situational, derived, and close to use.

### Context seeder

A context seeder is what brings a mandate lens into live use.

It carries selected parts of the work into a reasoning environment where they can start shaping how reasoning happens.

Loading a seeder is what shifts the working posture.
Reading about a lens does not.

### Public node

The public node is the place where the work can be inspected more fully.

It holds the materials, their visible history, and the paths through which the work changes.

It is not required for initial entry.
It exists for inspection, verification, and extension.

### Registers

Registers are controlled entry depths into the same reasoning path.

They change language, pacing, and entry point while preserving the structural
meaning of core terms, the main claims, and the reasoning path behind them.

Everyday starts from what people can already recognize in lived situations,
direct pressure, and visible consequences. It does that without reducing
meaning. Orientation helps a reader move through the structure step by step.
Practitioner carries the fullest trace, grounding, and working detail.

Register is not the same as language or locale. Locale changes the language of
the writing. Register changes how the same structure is presented.

They must not diverge in meaning.
They provide different ways into the same work.

For the fuller contract, read [Registers](/en-us/about/registers/).

### Practice of Clarity

The Practice of Clarity is a working discipline that keeps reasoning visible during decision formation.

It prioritizes traceability of judgment over speed of output.

It is not a framework, product, or methodology.
It is a constraint on how thinking is formed, expressed, and shared under pressure.

### Words you keep running into

#### Trace

You encounter trace when a claim is shown with its path instead of being left as a conclusion.

It is a visible record of what was decided, what was assumed, what remains uncertain, and how the claim will be checked.

This is not the same as technical tracing in software systems.

Tracing is the activity of making that record visible.

If a summary loses that path, it has lost the trace.

#### Inspection

You encounter inspection when a claim is checked against the path it leaves
behind.

Inspection is not blind distrust and it is not endless research. It is the
practice of asking whether a claim can be followed, questioned, bounded, and
corrected.

#### Structural truth

Structural truth is not a final truth owned by an authority.

It is the kind of truth that becomes more reliable when reasoning, assumptions,
limits, and corrections remain visible enough for others to inspect and revise.

#### Public reasoning infrastructure

Public reasoning infrastructure is the visible set of practices, records,
boundaries, and inspection paths that lets people follow how claims are formed,
where they stop, and how they can be challenged.

#### Node

You encounter a node when the practice has a bounded place to be carried with
visible reasoning, explicit limits, and trace others can inspect.

A team, project, community, translation, or repository can host a node. The team
itself is not the node by default.

What makes it a node is not the format. It is the fact that the practice is
being carried there with visible reasoning and explicit boundaries.

A public node is what you get when that work becomes inspectable on a public surface, such as a repository.

#### Bridge

You encounter a bridge when different readings, claims, or nodes need to stay connected without pretending they already agree.

A bridge makes divergence visible, names what is shared, and points toward the next check or bounded action.

It is not a forced consensus. It is connection without erasure.

#### Assumption

You encounter an assumption whenever a claim depends on something being treated as true, whether or not it has been said out loud.

Assumptions are not a flaw by themselves. They become a problem when they stay hidden and quietly steer the conclusion.

#### Boundary

You encounter a boundary when a piece of writing or reasoning names what it does not cover, what it did not verify, or where its claim stops holding.

Boundaries keep clarity from turning into overclaim.

They are how a text stays honest about its reach.

#### Interpretation

You encounter interpretation when the same writing starts being read, repeated, or applied in ways that change what it appears to mean.

Interpretation is expected.

The work is to notice when it materially shifts meaning and make that shift visible.

#### Lineage

You encounter lineage when you ask where an idea, method, or term came from, and how it changed as it moved.

Lineage keeps origin and mutation visible together.

It is not ownership theater. It is a way of tracing how meaning travels.

#### Drift

You encounter drift when the words of the practice stay in place but the discipline under them thins out.

Drift can look like trace language without an actual trace, boundary language without limits, or clarity language used as branding.

The task is not to panic about drift. It is to mark it before it hardens.

---

Gaps or ambiguities in this glossary indicate areas where the practice is still evolving.
