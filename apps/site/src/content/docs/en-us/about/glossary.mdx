This page defines the working language of the Practice of Clarity.

It exists to stabilize meaning across writing, lenses, and runtime use.

Definitions here are minimal by design. They are not exhaustive.
They set boundaries and prevent drift.

### Seeds

Seeds are the slow-changing layer of the practice.

They encode core distinctions, structural boundaries, and posture.
They are designed to remain stable across contexts and over time.

They are not context-specific and are not applied directly.
They make consistent application possible without re-deriving first principles.

### Mandate lenses

Mandate lenses are context-facing application layers.

They translate the stability of seeds into a specific domain or type of work.
They are expected to evolve through use and feedback.

They are not foundational artifacts.
They are derived, situational, and operational.

### Context seeder

A context seeder is the runtime entry point for a mandate lens.

It packages selected artifacts into a portable form that can be loaded into a reasoning environment.

Loading a seeder is what shifts the working posture.
Reading about a lens does not.

### Public node

The public node is the inspectable surface of the work.

It contains canonical artifacts, their lineage, and the structure through which they evolve.

It is not required for initial entry.
It exists for inspection, verification, and extension.

### Registers

Registers are controlled variations in presentation.

They change language, pacing, and accessibility while preserving structure, claims, and activation path.

They must not diverge in meaning.
They provide multiple entries into one system of thought.

### Practice of Clarity

The Practice of Clarity is a working discipline that keeps reasoning visible during decision formation.

It prioritizes traceability of judgment over speed of output.

It is not a framework, product, or methodology.
It is a constraint on how thinking is formed, expressed, and shared under pressure.

---

Gaps or ambiguities in this glossary indicate areas where the practice is still evolving.
