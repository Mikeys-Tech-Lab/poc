**Mikey Sebastian Drozd** <sup>he/him · they/them</sup>

My work sits around AI-augmented delivery, tech leadership, decision hygiene,
and organizational coherence under pressure.

The Practice of Clarity grew from a pressure that keeps showing up in the
current moment: work is speeding up, AI systems are entering ordinary
decisions, public debate keeps swinging between hype and panic, and many people
are being asked to trust outputs and decisions they cannot inspect.

In that pressure, capable people can act carefully, in good faith, and still
lose shared understanding when speed, hierarchy, incentives, and inherited
habits shape the room around them.

This work begins from one person's practice, research, lived experience, and
responsibility. It is not meant to stay centered on one person as it grows.

## How this work emerged

Since around 2019, I have been moving through a long process of unlearning how
certainty gets produced.

That meant looking at institutions, incentives, professional norms, inherited
narratives, and the quiet habits that teach people to sound clear before they
have actually understood what is happening.

It also meant learning to treat not knowing as a serious starting point.
Ignorance is not solved by sounding informed. It has to be traced: what is
known, what is assumed, what is missing, and what would need to be checked
before a claim can hold.

Before this public node existed, I had already been practicing this tracing in
my own work. Delivery work gave it a concrete testing ground: unclear
ownership, shifting context, delivery pressure, technical debt, organizational
incentives, and the everyday gap between what a system is and what people
believe it is.

Wider research helped me see those breakdowns as structural patterns, not only
local mistakes. It did not replace the work. It gave the work more context.

The arrival of powerful AI systems intensified the inquiry.

Generative tools can produce fluent, persuasive output without carrying the
reasoning path that would let people inspect how the output was formed. They
can also amplify existing patterns of thought, authority, exclusion, care, and
confusion. That made one thing unmistakable: people and teams can drift into
performance while still believing they are moving with understanding.

The Practice of Clarity emerged as a response to that condition.

It draws on delivery experience, structural thinking, metacognition, and the
discipline of not knowing too quickly. Those are not presented as special
authority. They are the conditions that made the practice possible.

It is a way of keeping reasoning visible while work, writing, and public claims
are still unfolding, so decisions remain examinable, uncertainty stays
speakable, and responsibility can be located without pretending certainty.

## Current practice

Since late 2025, I have been testing and revising the practice in public
through this site while also applying it inside AI-assisted delivery work.

> This public node is more than writing. It is a public rebuild of a practice I had already been carrying in private work: a way to make reasoning, source traces, assumptions, uncertainty, and responsibility visible enough to be inspected, questioned, and revised. The point is not to present a finished system. The point is to leave a clearer path than the one I had when I began.

The public site, repository, and supporting architecture show the practice while
it is still being tested, questioned, revised, and used. They do not claim that
the work is finished, universal, or above challenge. They show one attempt to
turn structural reasoning into something others can inspect instead of asking
them to trust a private opinion or polished conclusion.

This work continues wherever it is taken up and practiced.

My perspective is shaped by lived experience, including Slavic roots and queer
life. These shape how I read power, language, belonging, survival, and
institutional pressure. They are part of the ground I write from, not a claim
that the work belongs only to me or only to those experiences.

## Authorship

The authored content here is human-authored and AI-assisted.

Much of this work is spoken, reflected, drafted, revised, bounded, and shaped through disciplined practice. AI may assist with transcription, drafting, research, structure, and refinement, but it does not carry authorship or responsibility.

The judgment, boundaries, final decisions, and accountability remain human.

This note names one source of the work, not a central authority over it.
