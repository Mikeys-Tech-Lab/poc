**Mikey Sebastian Drozd** (he/him, they/them) is a Lead Consultant at Thoughtworks and a structural practitioner working on AI-augmented delivery, decision hygiene, and organisational coherence under pressure.

This site is the publication surface for the Practice of Clarity. The practice itself did not begin as a publication or a formal framework. It emerged from years of trying to understand why capable teams repeatedly lose shared understanding even when people are acting in good faith.

Since around 2019, Mikey has been moving through a long process of unlearning. That meant examining how reasoning is shaped by institutions, incentives, inherited narratives, and deeply normalized professional habits. It also meant refusing forms of performance that reward certainty over honesty, extraction over clarity, and speed over understanding.

What began as delivery experience evolved into a structural lens, one that is situated, globally conscious, and aware of its own limits. It recognizes that every perspective is partial, that Western technical culture carries its own distortions, and that clarity begins with acknowledging what we do not know.

From 2019 through late 2025, Mikey spent years tracing how systems shape decision behavior, how institutions normalize drift, and how shared reasoning breaks down under pressure.

The arrival of powerful AI systems intensified this inquiry. Generative tools can produce persuasive output while hallucinating reasoning. That made one thing unmistakable: teams can very easily drift into performance instead of understanding.

The Practice of Clarity emerged as a response. It is a way of making reasoning visible while work is still unfolding, so decisions remain examinable, uncertainty stays speakable, and responsibility can be located without pretending certainty.

Since late 2025, Mikey has been applying and refining this practice while enabling a high-performance engineering team operating inside an AI-accelerated delivery environment. The work focuses on governance, coherence, and responsible reasoning within large AI-generated codebases, while helping others sustain these disciplines under real scale-up pressure.

This perspective is also shaped by Slavic roots and queer life. These experiences inform the lens of the work without exhausting it.

This work is not presented as a finished system. The Practice of Clarity is something Mikey continues to test, revise, and practice in public. The aim is not to claim final answers, but to make reasoning visible while the work is still evolving and to invite others to practice the same discipline in their own environments.

The writing on this site is authored by a person. AI may assist with drafting and refinement inside disciplined practice, but judgment, responsibility, and authorship remain human.
