Registers are not a difficulty ladder.

They are a public contract for preserving the same reasoning path across
different entry depths.

That distinction matters. Public life, AI-assisted work, and institutional
decisions increasingly ask people to trust outputs whose reasoning path is
hidden, compressed, or unavailable. The Practice of Clarity responds by making
reasoning visible enough that readers can enter quickly, orient structurally,
and inspect more deeply without treating expertise as private authority.

The goal is not to make everyone do the same amount of research.

The goal is to make reasoning visible enough that people can enter at different
depths, trust provisionally, inspect when needed, and help revise the work when
the trace does not hold.

## Everyday

Everyday is the nearest honest doorway.

It starts from what people can already feel in the current moment:

- AI gives confident answers
- work moves too fast
- public claims arrive polished
- decisions ask for trust before their reasoning is visible
- not everyone has time, language, safety, or energy for a full source audit

Everyday is not a judgment about the reader.

It means the core pressure and the core claim are made recognizable without
forcing the reader to carry the full research burden first.

Everyday should sound human, direct, grounded, and close to life. It should not
sound like product documentation, education policy, or generic AI literacy
content.

## Orientation

Orientation carries readers from recognition into structure.

It can define terms such as trace, node, register, lens, public practice,
source boundary, and inspection, but only when those terms are doing work.

Orientation should say:

- this is what is happening
- this is the structure underneath it
- this is how the practice lets you move from pressure toward inspection

It connects everyday recognition to practitioner trace.

## Practitioner

Practitioner carries the fuller trace.

It can name architecture, sources, limits, public node, route, source hooks,
inspection surfaces, and governance boundaries. It should still read as public
reasoning infrastructure, not repository documentation.

Practitioner should answer:

- what is the claim?
- what supports it?
- what are its limits?
- where can it be inspected?
- what remains uncertain?
- what would count as drift?

Practitioner readers are not above everyday readers. They are doing more
inspection labor on the same public path.

## Drift risks

A register has drifted when it changes the claim instead of changing the
entry depth.

Common drift signs include:

- everyday becomes vague reassurance
- orientation becomes site explanation
- practitioner becomes repository inventory
- any register hides uncertainty to sound smoother
- any register treats the deeper layer as private authority

The repair is to reconnect the page to the shared path: claim, assumption,
boundary, trace, inspection, and revision.
