    <div data-register-content="everyday">
      <p>Things are moving fast. AI answers, work decisions, news, policies, and public claims arrive before people have time to understand what they are being asked to trust.</p>
      <p>The Practice of Clarity helps slow the claim down just enough to see the path behind it: what is known, what is assumed, what is still missing, and where to look next.</p>
    </div>
    <div data-register-content="orientation">
      <p>The Practice of Clarity is a public reasoning practice for keeping claims, assumptions, limits, and revisions visible under pressure.</p>
      <p>It lets readers begin from a pressure they recognize, move into structure, and follow the trace without treating private authority as the source of truth.</p>
    </div>
    <div data-register-content="practitioner">
      <p>The Practice of Clarity is public reasoning infrastructure for AI-assisted work and public decisions: registers, seeds, lenses, signals, and inspectable nodes that keep reasoning shareable under pressure.</p>
      <p>It treats AI as cognitive amplification that needs boundaries: source traces, assumptions, uncertainty, drift, and revision must stay visible so faster outputs can be challenged instead of becoming private authority.</p>
