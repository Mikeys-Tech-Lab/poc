This is where the series stops explaining and starts exposing.

Up to this point, the Acts named the condition, introduced the discipline, and showed how the structure holds across difference.

Now the question changes: what does it look like when a node actually lives?

### The shift

A repository is not proof by itself. It is a place where traces can remain visible.

It shows:

- what was written
- what changed
- what broke
- what remained unresolved

A polished article hides its scaffolding. A repository shows how that scaffolding holds or fails. A public node makes that exposure deliberate.

### What inspection makes possible

Inspection is structural access.

It allows a reader to:

- compare claims with their underlying trace
- see assumptions before they stabilize into language
- follow revisions across time
- detect where clarity holds and where it collapses

Without inspection, the practice risks becoming vocabulary. With inspection, it becomes accountable.

### Guided inspection

A public node should be readable in layers:

- inspect the [repository](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc)
- use the [onboarding guide](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc/blob/main/docs/onboarding/manual.md) for manual entry
- use [Evolution Arc](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc/blob/main/docs/onboarding/evolution-arc.md) for the reasoning trace behind the node

These surfaces do not replace the Acts. They show how the practice survives contact with real work.

### What this node reveals

A real node does not present itself as finished.

It contains:

- repository surfaces that show how decisions and structure evolved
- revision history that shows how meaning changed
- explicit boundaries where claims stop
- uncertainty that was not resolved before publication

### Seeds, repository, and practice

The seeds remain canonical grounding artifacts. They are not replaced by the node.

The repository shows how those definitions survive contact with reality. The public node is where the practice becomes inspectable. The next bridge, `Sensible Defaults`, is where that inspectable system becomes directly loadable in use.

### Boundaries

This repository is not:

- a canonical authority over future nodes
- a template to be copied without change
- a finished system
- a guarantee of correctness

Opening the node does not stabilize the practice. It exposes it to challenge.
