This is where the series stops explaining and starts exposing the work.

Up to this point, the Acts named the condition, introduced the discipline, and showed how the structure holds across difference.

Now the question changes: what does it look like when a node is public and in use?

### The shift

A repository is not proof by itself. It is a place where traces can stay visible.

It shows:

- what was written
- what changed
- what broke
- what remained unresolved

A polished article hides its scaffolding. A repository shows how that scaffolding holds or fails. A public node makes that exposure intentional.

<ReadingBeat>
Inspection turns the practice from vocabulary into accountable trace.
</ReadingBeat>

### What inspection makes possible

Inspection means structural access.

It allows a reader to:

- compare claims with their underlying trace
- see assumptions before they stabilize into language
- follow revisions across time
- detect where clarity holds and where it collapses

Without inspection, the practice risks becoming vocabulary. With inspection, it becomes accountable in practice.

### Guided inspection

A public node should be readable in layers:

- inspect the [repository](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc)
- if you want to look more closely, the repository includes a [manual reading path](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc/blob/main/docs/onboarding/manual.md)
- it also includes [Evolution Arc](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc/blob/main/docs/onboarding/evolution-arc.md) for following how the work evolved over time

These surfaces do not replace the Acts. They show how the practice holds under real work.

### What this node reveals

A real node does not present itself as finished or complete.

It contains:

- repository surfaces that show how decisions and structure evolved
- revision history that shows how meaning changed
- explicit boundaries where claims stop
- uncertainty that was not resolved before publication

### Seeds, repository, and practice

Seeds are writings that help the work keep its shape as it moves.
They are not replaced by the node.

The repository shows how those definitions hold under real conditions. The public node is where the practice becomes inspectable. `Sensible Defaults` is the first bridge from reading into use, pointing to a lens that can be tried in real work.
