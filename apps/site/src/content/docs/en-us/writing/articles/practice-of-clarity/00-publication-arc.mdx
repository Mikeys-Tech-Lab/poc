This page maps the visible sequence as it stands now.

AI has made generation cheap. It has not made judgment, integration, or verification cheap. If the repository or lens architecture arrives before the condition and discipline are clear, readers are likely to mistake the implementation for the practice.

<ReadingBeat>
  The order is part of the practice. Public understanding has to arrive before
  portable activation does.
</ReadingBeat>

### Sequence on the live surface

The main movement now visible on the live public surface is:

- `Home`
- `What This Work Is`
- `Architecture`
- `Act I`
- `Act II`
- `Act III`
- `Act IV`
- `Sensible Defaults`

`About the Author` and `CC BY 4.0` remain supporting framing surfaces around that sequence.

### Narrative build

1. `Act I` names the structural condition: output scales faster than shared understanding.
2. `Act II` introduces decision hygiene as the first usable discipline.
3. `Act III` shows how the practice can travel through nodes, bridges, and drift without collapsing into authority.
4. `Act IV` presents the repository as the public node that can be inspected rather than merely described.
5. `Sensible Defaults` is the first bridge article from the Acts into a canonical, loadable mandate lens package.

### System-layer mapping

- `Acts I-II` map the condition and discipline.
- `Act III` maps the structural layer of nodes, bridges, and drift.
- `Act IV` maps the repository node and public inspection surface.
- `Sensible Defaults` maps the first mandate lens and context seeder.
- `Home`, `What This Work Is`, and `Architecture` frame entry into the publication and the usable system.
- `seeds/`, `continuity/`, and `mandateLenses/` remain the root canonical surfaces.
