Work and delivery signals name the pressure that appears when software output
accelerates faster than the understanding around it.

AI sits inside that pressure in more than one role. It is a delivery tool that
produces code, text, and decisions quickly. It is also a system people work
under, and a system end users meet without choosing it.

### What belongs here

A work and delivery signal belongs here when it does three things:

1. names a delivery condition people can recognize
2. shows how AI changes the speed, cost, or confidence of that condition
3. keeps the reasoning inspectable instead of asserting a fix

The test is not whether a signal mentions AI. The test is whether it helps a
reader see a delivery structure that was already shaping their work, then judge
any practice against their own context.

### Integration Lag

The first series here is Integration Lag. It follows one delivery condition
across three signals: naming the gap between output and shared understanding, a
path back to inspectable work, and the verification cost that remains.
