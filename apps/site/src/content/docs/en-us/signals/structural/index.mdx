Structural signals are public entries for pressure patterns that need more than
a short definition.

They name a condition, show the assumptions beneath it, and keep enough of the
reasoning path visible for readers to inspect the claim.

They are not announcements.

They are not release notes.

They are not a content feed.

### What belongs here

A structural signal belongs here when it does three things:

1. names a condition people can recognize
2. exposes the assumptions beneath that condition
3. leaves a trace that can be inspected, challenged, or adapted

A structural signal may begin from AI, delivery, organizational pressure, public
practice, or the way understanding moves between people.

The test is not topic alone.

The test is whether the signal helps a reader see a structure that was already
shaping their work, public life, or decision environment.

Each signal should make a claim, show enough of its path, and keep the boundary
between observation and interpretation visible.

If a signal points toward practice, activation belongs after the reader has
enough context to judge whether that practice fits their situation.

### AI & Cognitive Amplification

The first structural signal cluster begins with:
<a href={ESSAY_HREF}>AI Is Not Magic. It Is a Cognitive Amplifier.</a>

It begins from the current public debate around AI and argues that AI works as
a cognitive amplifier: scaling reasoning-like outputs inside institutions
already shaped by power.

That first signal opens the cluster. It does not try to answer every question it
raises. Later signals can go deeper into the questions it makes visible: who
gets the time back, who supplied the work and memory, where the machine touches
the ground, what safety means beyond a filter, how public services can be
challenged, who writes the rules, and why the reasoning layer must remain
inspectable.

The sequence should stay with continuous public practice: trace, source
boundaries, provisional trust, and reasoning that can be inspected and revised.
