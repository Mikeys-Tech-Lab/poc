This piece focuses on the discipline of not knowing too fast.

It matters when uncertainty is present, but speed rewards premature certainty, polished language, or weak confidence theater.

### What it helps clarify

- how "I do not know yet" can stay rigorous
- why uncertainty needs structure, not embarrassment
- how openness can remain compatible with serious judgment

### When to read it

Read this when you want a deeper account of humility as practice rather than as
performance.
