title: What this is
description: A short introduction to the Practice of Clarity.
---

This is a living practice of clarity designed for federation.

It emerged from a simple observation: in many modern contexts, output can grow faster than shared understanding. Production accelerates. Distribution accelerates. But integration does not automatically keep pace.

The result is pressure, rework, fragmentation, and burnout.

This practice responds to that condition. It makes one structural shift: from content to trace.

## Trace

A trace is a short record of the reasoning path behind a claim: what was decided, what was assumed, what was uncertain, and how it will be checked.

Trace does not remove uncertainty. It makes uncertainty visible and shareable.

## Clarity

Clarity here does not mean certainty, agreement, or one final truth. It means:

- Assumptions are visible.
- Reasoning is inspectable.
- Sources are traceable.
- Context is declared.
- Differences are explicit rather than hidden.

## Federation

Federated means no single centre owns meaning. Anyone can begin where they are. Language can adapt to culture and domain. Connection is voluntary.

Each person, group, or community can form their own node of practice. Nodes do not need to merge or agree. They may connect through bridges: shared traces, shared definitions, shared translations.
