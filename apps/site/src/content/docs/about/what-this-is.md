This is a living practice of clarity designed for federation.

It emerged from a simple observation: in many modern contexts, output can grow faster than shared understanding. AI accelerates production. Media accelerates distribution. But integration does not automatically keep pace.

The result is pressure, rework, fragmentation, and burnout.

This practice responds to that condition. It makes one structural shift: from content to trace.

## Trace

A trace is a short record of the reasoning path behind a claim: what was decided, what was assumed, what was uncertain, and how it will be checked.

Trace does not remove uncertainty. It makes uncertainty visible and shareable.

## Clarity

Clarity here does not mean certainty, agreement, or one final truth. It means:

- Assumptions are visible.
- Reasoning is inspectable.
- Sources are traceable.
- Context is declared.
- Differences are explicit rather than hidden.

## Seeds

The practice is held by a small set of seed texts that define its discipline. These seeds are development-only sources and are not published directly as site content.

The site you are reading now evolves independently from the seeds, informed by them but not bound to reproduce them.
