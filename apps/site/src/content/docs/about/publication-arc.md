The Practice of Clarity is published in a structured sequence. This page explains the shape of that sequence and where we are in it.

## The arc

Publication follows a narrative structure with a prologue and four acts.

**Prologue** introduces the foundational idea: a practice of clarity designed for federation, not a product or a protocol.

**Act I** describes the condition that made the practice necessary. Production and distribution accelerate, but integration and reflection do not keep pace. The result is pressure.

**Act II** introduces the core discipline: trace over assertion. Claims expose their reasoning. Assumptions are visible. Uncertainty is acknowledged.

**Act III** explains how the practice scales without central control. Nodes of practice connect through bridges. Meaning accumulates without a center.

**Act IV** reveals the working implementation. Source history and implementation details are published with the repository when Act IV is released.

## Where we are now

The site you are reading is the reading surface of the practice. It publishes the concepts, the discipline, and the structural model.

The repository and implementation details will be published with Act IV. This is not missing trace. It is sequenced publication. The reasoning is visible here. The implementation follows.

## Why this sequence

Readers should understand the practice before encountering the repository that demonstrates it. Releasing both at once risks the implementation overshadowing the ideas it embodies.

The repository then functions as a living demonstration of the Practice of Clarity, not just a codebase.
