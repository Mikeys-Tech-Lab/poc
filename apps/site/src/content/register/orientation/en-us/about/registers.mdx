title: Registers
description: How the same reasoning path is entered at different depths.

Registers are not difficulty levels.

They are different entry depths into the same reasoning path.

That matters because public claims now move through AI-assisted work, public
debate, institutions, and teams faster than many people can inspect them. If a
claim asks for trust but hides the path behind it, the reader has to choose
between blind acceptance, endless research, or refusal.

The register system tries to make a better path possible.

It lets someone begin close to the pressure, move into structure, and inspect
the fuller trace without treating those steps as a hierarchy of readers.

## Everyday

Everyday begins from recognition.

It starts where the pressure is already felt: confident AI answers, fast-moving
work, polished public claims, unclear responsibility, and decisions people are
asked to trust before they can inspect the reasoning.

Everyday should be direct, human, and close to life.

It does not reduce the meaning. It reduces the entry burden.

## Orientation

Orientation turns recognition into structure.

It can define terms when they help the reader follow the practice: trace, node,
register, lens, source boundary, public practice, and inspection.

The job of orientation is to show what is happening, what structure is
underneath it, and how the practice lets a reader move from pressure toward
inspection.

## Practitioner

Practitioner carries the fuller trace.

It can name architecture, sources, limits, inspection surfaces, governance
boundaries, uncertainty, and drift risks.

Practitioner should answer:

- what is the claim?
- what supports it?
- what are its limits?
- where can it be inspected?
- what remains uncertain?
- what would count as drift?

Practitioner readers are not authorities over everyday readers. They are doing
more inspection labor on the same public path.

## What stays stable

The register changes the doorway, not the claim.

Each register should preserve the same structural direction:

- reasoning should be visible enough to follow
- assumptions should not be hidden
- uncertainty should not be polished away
- boundaries should stay named
- deeper inspection should remain possible
- challenge and revision should remain part of the work

This is what the registers protect: reasoning stays shareable without forcing
every reader to do the same amount of research before they can begin.
