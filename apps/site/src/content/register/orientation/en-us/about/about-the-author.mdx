**Mikey Sebastian Drozd** <sup>he/him · they/them</sup>

The Practice of Clarity grew from a pressure that keeps showing up in the
current moment: work is speeding up, AI systems are entering ordinary
decisions, public debate keeps swinging between hype and panic, and many people
are being asked to trust outputs and decisions they cannot inspect.

In that pressure, capable people can act carefully, in good faith, and still
lose shared understanding when speed, hierarchy, incentives, and inherited
habits shape the room around them.

This public node is one place where the practice is being rebuilt and made
inspectable through the public site, repository, and supporting architecture. It
begins from one person's work and responsibility. It is not meant to stay
centered on one person as it grows.

## How this work emerged

Since around 2019, I have been unlearning how certainty gets produced.

That meant learning to notice how institutions, class pressure, colonial inheritance, technology, professional common sense, and everyday survival habits affect what feels reasonable, urgent, or invisible.

The point was not to sound more informed.

It was to stop treating confident language as the same thing as understanding.

Before this public node existed, I had already been practicing this way of
tracing inside my own work. I was learning how to follow claims, assumptions,
uncertainty, and responsibility before I had the public structure for it.

Delivery work then gave the practice another concrete testing ground. In real work, clarity breaks in visible ways: decisions move faster than shared understanding, assumptions stay hidden, documentation falls behind, and people carry too much context in their heads.

Structural thinking, metacognition, and wider political research were part of
that practice. They helped me see those breakdowns as patterns, not isolated
failures. They are not presented here as special authority. They are part of
the conditions that made the practice possible.

When AI tools entered everyday work, the pattern became harder to ignore. These systems can produce confident answers without showing how they were formed. That makes it easy for people and teams to drift into performance while still believing they are moving with understanding.

The Practice of Clarity came out of that tension. It is a way to keep reasoning visible while work is still moving, so decisions can be followed, questioned, and understood.

## Current practice

Since late 2025, I have been testing and revising the practice in public,
while also applying it inside AI-assisted delivery work. The writing here
reflects the practice in motion, not a finished system.

This public node rebuilds a private practice in a form others can inspect,
question, and carry into their own contexts without having to begin from the
same fog.

My background includes Slavic roots and queer life. These shape how I read power, language, and survival. They are part of the ground I write from, not a claim that the work belongs only to me or only to those experiences.
