**Mikey Sebastian Drozd** <sup>he/him · they/them</sup>

This site is where the Practice of Clarity is written down and shared. The practice itself grew from years of trying to understand why people can be capable, careful, and committed, and still end up in confusion, rework, and drift.

## How this work emerged

Since around 2019, I have been going through a long process of unlearning. That meant looking closely at how institutions shape thinking, how pressure creates performative certainty, and how people often repeat systems they never consciously chose. It also meant refusing ways of working that treat people, including yourself, as machines for output instead of humans trying to think, decide, and act responsibly.

Over time, this changed the lens itself. The work became more structural, more globally conscious, and more honest about ignorance. It meant recognizing that Western institutions teach certain assumptions as if they were neutral truth, even when they are not, and that clarity begins by admitting the limits of my own perspective.

Before late 2025, I spent years tracing how decisions move through organisations, how power shapes behavior, and how shared understanding gets lost under urgency.

The rise of AI tools made this even sharper. These systems can generate convincing answers while inventing reasoning behind the scenes. That made it clear: work can easily become performance instead of understanding.

Out of that came the Practice of Clarity: a way I use to make reasoning easier to see while work is happening, so people can understand why decisions were made, what assumptions are in play, and where uncertainty still lives.

## Current practice

Since late 2025, I have been exploring and stress-testing these ideas while helping a high-performance engineering team work safely inside an AI-accelerated delivery environment.

My background includes Slavic roots and queer life. These experiences shape the perspective of the work without becoming the whole story.

This work is not finished, and it is not meant to be. The Practice of Clarity is something I continue to explore in public while the work is still unfolding. Others are welcome to recognize its value and begin practicing it in their own teams and communities.

## Authorship

The writing on this site is written by a person. AI may assist with drafting, but the thinking, judgment, and responsibility remain human.
