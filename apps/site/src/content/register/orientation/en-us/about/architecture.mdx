### The parts

- Public writing: where the condition and the practice can be entered
- Mandate lenses: where the practice becomes specific to a kind of work
- Context seeders: where a lens is brought into a working environment
- Public node (for example, the [repository](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc)): where the work can be inspected, evolved further, forked, and contributed to over time

### What each part does

#### Public writing

It lets someone meet the condition, the language, and the practice without having to do anything with it yet. Its job is to help the work become understandable.

#### Mandate lenses

They take what stays steady in the practice and bring it closer to a real situation. They are not the base of the work. They are the part that helps the work become usable.

#### Context seeders

They carry selected parts of the practice into a place where reasoning is
already happening. That is when the practice stops being only something you
read and starts changing how the reasoning itself is carried, questioned, and
revised.

#### Public node

It includes the repository, the materials, and the visible history of how the work changes. It is there for when you want to look more closely at how the work is held, checked, and evolved over time.

The repository is one place where this work is made visible. It is not the only
place it can exist.

These parts are connected, but they do different work.

Public writing helps you enter. Mandate lenses help you apply. Context seeders make the practice present during the work. The public node lets you inspect how it is built and how it changes.
