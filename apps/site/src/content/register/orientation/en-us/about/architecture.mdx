This page shows how the main parts of the work fit together, and why they are
connected without all doing the same job.

That separation matters because public reasoning needs entry, grounding, use,
and inspection to stay connected without becoming one blurred surface.

### The parts

**Public surfaces**  
Where the condition, the writing, and the practice can be entered.

**Registers**  
Where the same reasoning path is entered at different depths.

**Seeds**  
Where the reasoning-contract texts of the practice are held and kept stable over time.

**Mandate lenses**  
Where the practice becomes specific to a kind of work.

**Context seeders**  
Where relevant source context is carried into a live reasoning environment.

**Public node**  
Where the work can be inspected, evolved further, and made visible over time.  
Here, the public node is the public site and repository together: the site is
where most readers enter the work, and the repository keeps more of the trace
inspectable.

### What each part does


#### Public surfaces

They let someone meet the condition, the language, and the practice without
having to do anything with it yet. Their job is to help the work become
understandable and followable.

#### Registers

They keep the public surfaces connected across Everyday, Orientation, and
Practitioner depths. The register changes the entry point, not the claim. The
shared rule is named in [Registers](/en-us/about/registers/).

#### Seeds

They hold the reasoning-contract texts that keep the practice stable.

They make visible how reasoning is formed, where limits sit, and how the work stays steady as it moves across situations.

They are written to be readable by people and usable by AI as part of a source context. In practice, they shape how responses stay grounded.

They do not activate anything on their own.

#### Mandate lenses

They take what stays steady in the practice and bring it into a real situation. They are not the base of the work. They are what makes the practice usable in context.

#### Context seeders

They carry selected source context into a place where reasoning is already happening. This is where the practice stops being only something you read and starts shaping how decisions are made, questioned, and revised inside a live reasoning environment.

#### Public node

It holds the materials and the visible history of how the work changes. It is there for when you want to look more closely at how the work is held, checked, and evolved over time, and to keep that evolution visible and inspectable.

Here, the public node is the site and repository together. The site is where most readers enter the work. The repository is where more of the structure, history, source surfaces, and supporting material can be inspected.

These parts are connected, but they do different work.

Public surfaces help you enter. Registers keep that entry connected to deeper
inspection. Seeds keep the reasoning contract stable. Mandate lenses move that
contract toward use. Context seeders carry relevant source context into live
reasoning work. The public node lets you inspect how it is built and how it
changes.
