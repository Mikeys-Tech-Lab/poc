This map shows how the main parts of the work relate, and why they are
connected without all doing the same job.

That separation matters because public reasoning needs entry, grounding, use,
and inspection to stay connected without becoming one blurred surface.

### The parts

**Public writing**  
Where the condition and the practice can be entered.

**Registers**
Where the same reasoning path is entered at different depths.

**Seeds**  
Where the core texts of the practice are held and kept stable over time.

**Mandate lenses**  
Where the practice becomes specific to a kind of work.

**Context seeders**  
Where a lens is brought into a working environment.

**Public node**  
Where the work can be inspected, evolved further, and made visible over time.  
One example is the [repository](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc). A
team, project, or community can host a node, but the node is the bounded trace
surface, not the people by default.

### What each part does


#### Public writing

It lets someone meet the condition, the language, and the practice without
having to do anything with it yet. Its job is to help the work become
understandable.

#### Registers

They keep the public writing connected across everyday, orientation, and
practitioner depths. The register changes the entry point, not the claim. The
full contract is named in [Registers](/en-us/about/registers/).

#### Seeds

They hold the core texts that keep the practice stable.

They make visible how reasoning is formed, where limits sit, and how the work stays steady as it moves across situations.

They are written to be readable by people and usable by AI at the same time. In practice, they shape how responses stay grounded.

They do not activate anything on their own.

#### Mandate lenses

They take what stays steady in the practice and bring it into a real situation. They are not the base of the work. They are what makes the practice usable in context.

#### Context seeders

They carry selected parts of the practice into a place where reasoning is already happening. This is where the practice stops being only something you read and starts shaping how decisions are made, questioned, and revised in real time.

#### Public node

It holds the materials and the visible history of how the work changes. It is there for when you want to look more closely at how the work is held, checked, and evolved over time, and to keep that evolution visible and inspectable.

The repository is one place where this work is made visible. It is not the only place it can exist.

These parts are connected, but they do different work.

Public writing helps you enter. Registers keep that entry connected to deeper
inspection. Mandate lenses help you apply. Context seeders bring the practice
into the work. The public node lets you inspect how it is built and how it
changes.
