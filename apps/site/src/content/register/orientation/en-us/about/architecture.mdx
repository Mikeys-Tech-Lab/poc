### The parts

**Public writing**  
Where the condition and the practice can be entered.

**Seeds**  
Where the core texts of the practice are held in stable form.

**Mandate lenses**  
Where the practice becomes specific to a kind of work.

**Context seeders**  
Where a lens is brought into a working environment.

**Public node**  
Where the work can be inspected, evolved further, and made visible over time.  
One example is the [repository](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc), but a node can take different forms depending on where the practice is carried.

### What each part does


#### Public writing

It lets someone meet the condition, the language, and the practice without having to do anything with it yet. Its job is to help the work become understandable.

#### Seeds

They hold the core texts that keep the practice stable.

They explain how reasoning is made visible, where limits sit, and how the work stays steady as it moves across different situations.

They are written so both people and AI systems can understand them.  
They help shape how thinking and responses stay grounded.

They are not tied to one situation and do not activate anything on their own.

#### Mandate lenses

They take what stays steady in the practice and bring it closer to a real situation. They are not the base of the work. They are the part that helps the work become usable.

#### Context seeders

They carry selected parts of the practice into a place where reasoning is
already happening. That is when the practice stops being only something you
read and starts changing how the reasoning itself is carried, questioned, and revised in real time.

#### Public node

It holds the materials and the visible history of how the work changes. It is there for when you want to look more closely at how the work is held, checked, and evolved over time, and to keep that evolution visible and inspectable.

The repository is one place where this work is made visible. It is not the only
place it can exist.

These parts are connected, but they do different work.

Public writing helps you enter. Mandate lenses help you apply. Context seeders make the practice present during the work. The public node lets you inspect how it is built and how it changes.
