Work can now move very quickly.

This is not only because of AI.

Work has been moving faster for a long time.

AI just makes it easier to produce answers that look complete before they are fully understood.

But when things move faster, it becomes easier to lose track of why decisions were made.

This is where a different way of working becomes important.

### The idea

Instead of focusing only on the result, teams also keep track of:

- what they are assuming
- what they are unsure about
- what might change later

This is called decision hygiene.

It means keeping the thinking visible while work is still happening.

Without this, teams often keep going even when something feels unclear.

Questions get skipped because there is no time.  
People agree to keep things moving.

The work continues.

Understanding falls behind.

### Example

A plan looks clear, so everyone agrees and starts working.

Later, problems appear.

Not because people made mistakes, but because each person understood the plan slightly differently.

The plan was shared.  
The understanding was not.

### What changes

Teams begin to ask small questions during the work:

- what are we assuming here?
- what do we not know yet?

These are not formal steps.

They are part of how work moves forward.

This creates a tension that cannot be solved by rules or policies.

The thinking needs to be visible so people can understand each other, but it should not turn into something people perform or report on.

If it becomes something to complete or prove, people will focus on looking clear instead of actually being clear.

This way of working cannot be forced from the outside.

It has to happen inside the work itself.

### What this is not

This is not about more process or more documentation.

It may feel different at first, because things that were implicit are now spoken out loud.

Over time, it often reduces confusion, even though the path there is not always smooth.

It should make work clearer, not slower.

It is also not about proving that you are right.

If people feel they need to justify themselves, the practice has already shifted into performance.

### What comes next

Once reasoning is visible, another question appears:

How does that reasoning move through a larger system?

That is the question we take up next: how do we keep this understanding alive as it moves between people and teams?
