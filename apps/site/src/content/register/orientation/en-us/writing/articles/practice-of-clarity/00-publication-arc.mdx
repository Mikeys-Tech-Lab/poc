This page shows the release sequence for the Practice of Clarity.

The order matters because the practice should be understandable before the implementation becomes the loudest thing in the room.

### Sequence on the live surface

The main movement now visible on the live public surface is:

- `Home`
- `What This Work Is`
- `Architecture`
- `Act I`
- `Act II`
- `Act III`
- `Act IV`
- `Sensible Defaults`

`About the Author` and `CC BY 4.0` support the sequence without replacing it.

### How the movement builds

1. `Act I` names the condition: output is moving faster than shared understanding.
2. `Act II` introduces the discipline: keep reasoning visible while work is still moving.
3. `Act III` explains how the practice travels through nodes, bridges, and drift without central ownership.
4. `Act IV` opens the repository as a public node you can inspect.
5. `Sensible Defaults` becomes the first bridge from reading into use.

### Why `Sensible Defaults` comes after `Act IV`

It is not another Act.

It is the first practical entry point that lets someone move from understanding the practice to loading a lens and trying it in real work.
