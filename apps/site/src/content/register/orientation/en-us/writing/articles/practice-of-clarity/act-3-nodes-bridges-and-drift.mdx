When a useful idea spreads, people use it in different ways.

Some apply it carefully. Some misunderstand it. Some keep the words but lose the meaning.

So the question becomes: how can people stay connected without one person deciding what everything means?

<ReadingBeat>
  The problem is not difference. The problem is difference that nobody can
  inspect anymore.
</ReadingBeat>

### Nodes

A node is a place where the practice is actually used: a team, a group, or a project.

### Bridges

A bridge is a way to make differences between nodes visible.

It can show:

- how two teams approached the same problem
- what each one assumed
- what remained unclear

The goal is not to force agreement. The goal is to help people understand each other.

### Drift

Drift happens when the words stay the same, but the thinking behind them becomes less clear.

The problem is not that drift happens. The problem is when no one can see it anymore.

Instead of arguing definitions, people can show their work:

- what they did
- what they assumed
- what they are still unsure about

This makes comparison possible without a central authority.
