This reading explains why the practice does not stop at having an answer.

It stays with how the answer was formed, what it leaves out, and whether others
can still question it together.

It also sets an important boundary: this is a living practice, not a protocol,
software standard, product, or belief system. The public site is the primary
reader-facing surface. The repository is the inspectable operational node behind
it.

Federation describes what this practice could become if others adopt it. It is
not a claim that a multi-node community exists now.

### What it helps you see

- why trace matters more than polish
- why clarity is something you practice, not something you perform
- why fast output can still leave people less able to understand together
- why the public site is the main reading surface
- why the repository is the inspectable source behind it
- why federation is possible later, but not claimed as active now

### When to read it

Read this when you want to understand the pressure behind the practice, why the
work keeps returning to visible reasoning, and how one public node can stay
inspectable without pretending to be a finished system.
