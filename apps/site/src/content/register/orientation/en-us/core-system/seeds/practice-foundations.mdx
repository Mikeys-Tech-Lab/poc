This reading names the boundaries that keep the work recognizable as it moves.

It makes clearer what the practice is committed to, what would make it stop
being itself, and how adaptation can stay accountable without becoming ownership
theater.

It also sets a safety boundary: this practice documents reasoning, not people.
It is not a data collection program, surveillance layer, compliance tool, or
substitute for engineering work.

The public site is the primary reading surface. The repository remains the
inspectable source of authorship, history, and licensing.

### What it helps you see

- what the practice depends on
- what kinds of use would distort or weaken it
- why trace must document reasoning, not people
- why the practice must not become surveillance, coercive enforcement, or compliance theater
- how boundaries keep the work from turning into performance or control
- why the public site and repository do different jobs in keeping the work readable and inspectable

### When to read it

Read this when you want a stronger sense of what keeps the practice intact,
especially as it starts being shared, adapted, or applied in new places without
losing its boundaries.
