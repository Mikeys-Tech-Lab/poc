When a useful practice moves between contexts, people use it in different ways.

Some apply it carefully. Some misunderstand it. Some keep the words but lose the meaning.

The question becomes: how can people stay connected without one place deciding what everything means?

<ReadingBeat>
  The problem is not difference. The problem is difference that cannot be inspected anymore.
</ReadingBeat>

### Nodes

A node is not simply a team or group.

It is a bounded place where the practice is carried with visible reasoning,
clear limits, and enough trace for someone else to inspect.

Later, another team, group, or project could host its own node when it leaves that kind of trace.

### Bridges

A bridge is a way to make differences between nodes, or between contexts beginning to carry the practice, visible and comparable.

It can show:

- how two working contexts approached the same problem
- what each one assumed
- what remained unclear

The goal is not to force agreement. The goal is to help people understand each other without erasing the difference or pretending one center owns the meaning.

### Drift

Drift happens when the words stay the same, but the reasoning behind them becomes thinner or less clear.

The problem is not that drift happens. The problem is when no one can see it anymore.

Instead of arguing over definitions, people can show their work:

- what they did
- what they assumed
- what they are still unsure about

This makes comparison possible without a central authority.
