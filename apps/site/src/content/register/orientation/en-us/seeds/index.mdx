These are interconnected reasoning seeds.

They were written to help reasoning stay more grounded, inspectable, and less
likely to drift as the practice moves across contexts.

They matter especially in situations where reasoning-capable models are part of
the work, but they are not limited to those situations.

You do not need them before you begin.

Come here when you want more grounding, more language, or more of the writing
that helps the practice keep its shape. More seeds will come over time.

### What this section is for

This is not the first step.

It is a place to go deeper when you want to understand the writings that help
reasoning stay steadier as the work moves across different situations, readers,
or forms of use.
