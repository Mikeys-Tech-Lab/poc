Many people are now expected to work, decide, and respond faster than they can really understand what is happening. When that happens, pressure rises, confusion spreads, and responsibility lands on people without enough shared clarity.

Practice of Clarity is a publication about that condition and about a practical response to it: making reasoning visible so people can follow how decisions were formed, what is still uncertain, and where the pressure is landing.
