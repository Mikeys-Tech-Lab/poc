## A shorter way in

Work can move faster than understanding.

That is the beginning of integration lag.

A team may be shipping, closing tickets, writing code, and producing more with AI assistance. From the outside, it can look like momentum.

Inside the work, people may still feel something else:

- reviews are heavier
- hidden assumptions appear late
- decisions are harder to explain
- people agree without understanding the same thing
- AI drafts create more checking work than expected

Integration lag names that gap.

## What the gap is

Integration lag is the delay between producing change and integrating that change into shared understanding.

It is not only about CI/CD.

It is about whether the people involved can name the evidence, assumptions, risk, and human decision behind the work in roughly the same way.

If they cannot, the team may have visible agreement without shared understanding.

## Why AI makes it easier to miss

AI can make drafts arrive quickly.

That is useful.

But a fluent draft can look finished before the system has been inspected.

A generated answer can sound confident before the evidence is visible.

A generated test can look reassuring before it proves the right behavior.

So the issue is not only whether AI is wrong.

The issue is that AI can make unresolved uncertainty look complete.

## A practical test

Before the work hardens, ask:

> Can we name what this change stands on?

That means:

- what evidence was inspected
- what assumptions were made
- what remains uncertain
- what checks matter
- what decision still belongs to a human

The goal is not more process.

The goal is to make the work easier to inspect before it becomes expensive to change.
