Work and delivery signals look at what happens when software ships faster than
the people around it can keep up.

AI plays more than one part here. It is a tool teams use to produce work
quickly. It is also a system people end up working under, and one that reaches
end users who never chose it.

### What belongs here

A signal fits here when it:

1. names a delivery situation you can recognize
2. shows how AI changes its speed, cost, or confidence
3. keeps its reasoning open instead of selling a fix

The point is not that a signal mentions AI. The point is whether it helps you
see a delivery pattern already shaping your work, so you can judge any practice
in your own setting.

### Integration Lag

The first series here is Integration Lag. It follows one situation across three
signals: naming the gap between output and shared understanding, a path back to
work you can inspect, and the checking cost that stays.
