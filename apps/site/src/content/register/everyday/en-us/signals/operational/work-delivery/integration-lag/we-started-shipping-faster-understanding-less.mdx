## The problem people keep running into

Work got faster.

AI can help write, summarize, explain, plan, compare, and draft.

That can be useful.

But faster work does not automatically mean clearer work.

A team can ship more and still understand less about what is happening.

That gap is integration lag.

## What it feels like

It often feels ordinary at first:

- everyone is busy
- tickets are moving
- reviews are harder
- small surprises keep appearing
- people agree in meetings but mean different things
- one person has to keep all the context in their head

Nothing has to explode for this to be real.

The cost often appears later.

## A simple way to say it

Integration lag means:

> the work moved faster than shared understanding.

The question is not only whether the work was produced.

The question is whether people can explain what it means, what it depends on, and
what still needs checking.

If only one person can explain the change, understanding has not really landed
with the team yet.

If the work needs a long private explanation before anyone can review it,
something important is still missing from the shared surface.

## Why AI matters here

AI can make answers look complete before they are grounded.

A confident answer can still be missing local context.

A working-looking draft can still rely on the wrong assumption.

That does not mean AI is useless.

It means the useful move is not blind trust or blanket rejection.

The useful move is inspection.

Before accepting the draft, slow down just enough to ask what the draft is
standing on.

That is different from stopping the work.

It is how the work stays understandable while it moves.

## Try this question

Before accepting a draft, ask:

> what evidence does this stand on?

Then ask:

- what was checked?
- what was assumed?
- what is still uncertain?
- what decision belongs to a person?

That is the beginning of reducing integration lag.

## The next register

This is the doorway into the signal.

It starts from the feeling many people recognize: the work is moving, but the
meaning is harder to hold together.

The next layer names more of the structure: output, evidence, assumptions,
checks, and human decision.

The fullest layer keeps the full trace, source surface, and working detail.

The claim should stay connected across all three. The language changes so people
can enter at different depths; the responsibility to leave a visible path does
not.

>
