## The problem people keep running into

Work got faster.

AI can help write, summarize, explain, plan, and draft.

That can be useful.

But faster work does not automatically mean clearer work.

A team can ship more and still understand less about what is happening.

That gap is integration lag.

## What it feels like

It may feel like:

- everyone is busy
- tickets are moving
- reviews are harder
- small surprises keep appearing
- people agree in meetings but mean different things
- one person has to keep all the context in their head

Nothing has to explode for this to be real.

The cost often appears later.

## A simple way to say it

Integration lag means:

> the work moved faster than shared understanding.

The question is not only whether the work was produced.

The question is whether people can explain what it means, what it depends on, and what still needs checking.

## Why AI matters here

AI can make answers look complete before they are grounded.

A confident answer can still be missing local context.

A working-looking draft can still rely on the wrong assumption.

So the useful move is not blind trust or blanket rejection.

The useful move is inspection.

## Try this question

Before accepting a draft, ask:

> what evidence does this stand on?

Then ask:

- what was checked?
- what was assumed?
- what is still uncertain?
- what decision belongs to a person?

That is the beginning of reducing integration lag.
