Sometimes a page asks you to trust that the work is careful.

This practice asks for something better than trust alone. It asks for a way to look behind the claim.

That is what this page means by inspection.

The public repository is one place where you can see more of the work. It can show pages, guidance, and change over time. It cannot prove everything by itself.

### What you are looking for

You are not looking for a perfect archive.

You are looking for whether the work leaves a trail.

Can you see what the page is claiming? Can you see where that claim is held? Can you see what the work says it does not cover? Can you see when something changed?

Those questions matter because clear work should not depend only on a polished page.

### A simple way to inspect

Pick one claim.

Then ask:

- What is this page saying?
- Where would I expect that to be visible?
- Does the visible surface support it?
- What is still unclear?

That is enough to begin.

You do not need to check everything. Checking everything usually creates noise.

The public repository is available at [github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc). It is there as a trace surface, not as a final authority.

### What the repository can and cannot do

The repository can show that something exists.

It can show that a page, guidance file, or change history is public.

It can help you follow how the work is held together.

But it cannot decide the meaning for you. It cannot prove that every claim is complete. It cannot replace careful reading.

Inspection still needs judgment.

<ReadingBeat>
  The useful question is not "is there a repository?" The useful question is
  "does the visible trace help me understand and question the claim?"
</ReadingBeat>

### If something feels off

If a page says one thing and the visible trace seems to say another, do not rush to smooth it over.

Name the difference.

That difference may be a mistake, a missing explanation, or a sign that the work has changed and the public surface has not caught up.

In this practice, that kind of mismatch is not something to hide. It is something to mark before it becomes drift.
