Work moves faster than people can make sense of it together.

Answers arrive before people can ask what they rest on, what is still missing,
or who carries the risk if they are wrong.

This work is for that pressure: a way to make reasoning shareable before it
turns into a polished conclusion people are asked to trust.

### What this is

It is a practice for keeping reasoning visible while decisions, claims, and
explanations are still being formed.

Not later as cleanup.
Not only as documentation.
While the work is still moving.

It helps people ask and show:

- what they are assuming
- what they actually know
- what is still uncertain
- what needs checking next

This doorway stays close to the pressure people can already feel. The same
claim can be followed further into structure and inspection.

### What this changes

The point is not to sound smarter or more careful.

The point is to make thinking visible enough that other people can question it,
revise it, and work with it before it hardens into a decision.

Not everyone needs to inspect every source before they begin. But everyone
should be able to see that a claim has a path, that the path has limits, and
that deeper inspection remains possible.

For example, if a team says a deadline can move forward, this practice asks:

- what are we assuming?
- what has not been checked yet?
- what would need to happen before that date is real?

### What this is not

This is not a demand to explain everything all the time.

- It is not surveillance.
- It is not a performance of certainty.
- It is not pressure to sound finished before the work is ready.

If making reasoning visible turns into exposure, extraction, or control, it has
left the practice.

<StillEvolvingNote>
  This work is not presented as finished. It is being tested, revised, and
  practiced in public. The aim is not to claim final answers, but to keep
  reasoning visible while the work is still evolving, so others can work this
  way in their own contexts.
</StillEvolvingNote>
