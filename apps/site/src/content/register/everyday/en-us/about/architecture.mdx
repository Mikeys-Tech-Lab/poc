This map shows how the main parts fit together without turning them into one
thing.

The parts stay separate so a reader can see where the work begins, where it
gets steadier, where it moves into use, and where it can be inspected.

### The parts

**Public writing**  
Where people first meet the work.

**Registers**
Where the same reasoning path gets different entry depths.

**Seeds**  
Where the core texts are kept steady.

**Mandate lenses**  
Where the practice is turned toward a kind of work.

**Context seeders**  
Where a lens is brought into a live working environment.

**Public node**  
Where the work can be inspected in public over time.

### Why the parts stay separate

These parts are connected, but they do different jobs.

Public writing opens the door.
Registers keep that door connected to deeper inspection.
Seeds keep the language steady.
Mandate lenses bring the practice toward use.
Context seeders bring it into live work.
The public node lets people inspect how it is being carried and changed.

One example of a public node is the
[repository](https://github.com/Mikeys-Tech-Lab/poc).

A team, project, or community can host a node, but the node is the visible trace
surface, not the people by default.

When those roles blur, the work gets harder to read.

A map helps keep them distinct without pretending they are isolated.
