**Mikey Sebastian Drozd** <sup>he/him · they/them</sup>

This work started from a simple pressure: people are asked to trust confident
answers, fast decisions, and polished claims before they can see what those
claims rest on.

I have spent years trying to understand why careful, capable people can still
lose shared understanding under pressure.

## How this work emerged

Since around 2019, I have been unlearning how easily confidence can be mistaken
for truth.

That meant looking at how institutions shape thinking, how pressure rewards
fast answers over honest ones, and how people repeat patterns they never really
chose.

Over time, the focus shifted from opinion to structure.

Less on sounding certain.
More on staying accountable to what is known, what is assumed, and what is not
yet clear.

Before this public rebuild existed, I had already been practicing this in my
own work: tracing claims, checking assumptions, and learning not to trust an
answer just because it sounded complete.

When AI tools entered everyday work and everyday life, that pressure became
harder to ignore.

A chatbot can help you begin.

It can also give you a smooth answer before you know what was assumed, what was
left out, or what still needs checking.

The Practice of Clarity grew out of that problem.

It is a way of keeping the path visible: what is known, what is assumed, what is
uncertain, and what still needs to be checked.

## Current practice

This private practice is now being rebuilt in public.

I had to learn much of it the hard way. PoC leaves a clearer public trail so
others can inspect it, question it, and begin with more support than I had.

The writing here shows the practice while it is still being refined.

My background includes Slavic roots and queer life. That shapes how I read
power, language, and survival. It is part of the ground I write from, not a
boundary around who the work is for.
