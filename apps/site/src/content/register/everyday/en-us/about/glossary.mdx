This glossary keeps plain meanings for the words that keep coming back.

Use it when a term starts doing more work than it first seemed to.

### Main parts

#### Seeds

Seeds are reasoning-contract texts that keep the work steady.

They hold the language, limits, and reasoning discipline that the rest of the
practice depends on.

#### Mandate lenses

Mandate lenses bring the practice toward a specific kind of work.

They take what stays steady in the seeds and turn it toward a real situation.

#### Context seeder

A context seeder carries relevant source context into a live reasoning
environment.

Reading about a lens is not the same as loading source context. Source context
is what carries the practice into action.

#### Public node

A public node is where the work can be inspected in public.

Here, that means the public site and repository together: the site is where most
readers enter, and the repository keeps more of the trace visible.

#### Registers

Registers are different doorways into the same work.

Everyday starts with what people can already recognize. Orientation carries
readers from recognition into structure. Practitioner carries the fullest trace.

Register is not the same as language or locale. Locale changes the language of
the writing. Register changes how the same work is entered.

For the fuller register rule, read [Registers](/en-us/about/registers/).

#### Practice of Clarity

The Practice of Clarity is a way of keeping reasoning visible while work is
still moving.

It helps people question, revise, and share reasoning before decisions harden.

### Words you keep running into

#### Trace

Trace is what lets you see how someone got to a claim.

It shows what was assumed, what was seen, what is still uncertain, and what
needs checking next.

#### Inspection

Inspection is what happens when someone checks a claim against the path it
leaves behind.

It is not suspicion for its own sake. It is how trust stays connected to what
can be followed, questioned, and corrected.

#### Structural truth

Structural truth is not a final answer someone owns.

It is what becomes more reliable when a claim keeps its reasoning, assumptions,
limits, and corrections visible enough for others to inspect.

#### Public reasoning infrastructure

Public reasoning infrastructure means the visible paths that help people follow
how a claim was formed, where it stops, and how it can be challenged.

#### Node

A node is a bounded place where the practice is carried with visible reasoning,
limits, and trace.

A team, project, translation, or repository can host a node. The team itself is
not the node by default.

#### Bridge

A bridge keeps two readings, claims, or nodes connected without pretending they
already agree.

It names what is shared, what is different, and what needs checking next.

#### Assumption

An assumption is something a claim depends on, even if nobody has said it out
loud.

Assumptions become risky when they stay hidden and quietly steer the result.

#### Boundary

A boundary names what a claim does not cover, what was not checked, or where
the claim stops holding.

Boundaries keep clarity from turning into overclaim.

#### Interpretation

Interpretation is what happens when the same writing starts landing differently
in different readers, settings, or uses.

That is expected. The work is to notice when the shift changes the meaning in a
way that needs to be named.

#### Lineage

Lineage asks where an idea, term, or method came from, and what changed as it
moved.

It keeps origin and change visible together.

#### Drift

Drift is what happens when the language of the practice stays in place but the
care underneath it starts thinning out.

That can look like using the word `trace` without showing any path, or talking
about clarity without naming assumptions, limits, or uncertainty.

If something is missing or unclear here, that is part of the work still
evolving.
