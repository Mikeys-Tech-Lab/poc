## Purpose

This document anchors the structure of the system.

It exists to:

- maintain continuity over time
- prevent drift while evolving
- clarify what each layer is responsible for
- ensure the system remains inspectable, not abstract

This is not a seed.  
This is not a mandate lens.  

This is a continuity anchor.

---

## The system layers

### 1. Seeds

**Role:**  
Deep structural anchors.

**Characteristics:**
- slow to change  
- not owned  
- not centrally controlled  
- inspectable and traceable  
- grounded in structural analysis  

**Function:**
Seeds provide **shared reference points**, not authority.

They are not a constitution.  
They are not doctrine.  

They exist so that:
- different contexts can remain grounded without central control  
- reasoning can be traced back to structural patterns  

---

### 2. Practice of Clarity

**Role:**  
Method layer.

**Function:**
Defines how reasoning is made:
- visible  
- traceable  
- inspectable  

It ensures:
- assumptions are not hidden  
- conclusions can be followed  
- disagreement remains legible  

**Important:**
Practice does not enforce truth.

It makes reasoning visible so that:
- distortion can be detected  
- alignment can be negotiated  
- clarity can emerge without authority  

---

### 3. Mandate Lenses

**Role:**  
Contextual application layer.

**Characteristics:**
- fast-evolving  
- community-driven  
- domain-specific  
- not centrally governed  

**Function:**
Mandate lenses apply seeds within real contexts.

They:
- interpret structural patterns in specific environments  
- adapt to local constraints  
- evolve through use  

**Constraint:**
Every mandate lens must:
- expose its reasoning  
- remain traceable to seeds  
- operate through the Practice of Clarity  

---

## Portable context seeding

Mandate lenses may be distributed in portable forms  
(e.g. prompts, context seeders, or shared agent configurations).

These forms act as lightweight entry points that allow a lens
to be applied instantly without installation or setup.

Bridge articles may also act as entry surfaces.
They can encode register, tone, vocabulary density, audience framing, and an
immediate starting action.

**Function:**
- seed the lens into an active context  
- establish sensible defaults immediately  
- make reasoning expectations visible from the start  

**Constraints:**
- must remain traceable to the canonical source  
- must not replace the continuity layer  
- must not obscure or compress reasoning  

Portable context seeding depends on canonical artifacts being directly readable
on their primary surface. See:
[`canonical-readability-requirement.md`](./canonical-readability-requirement.md)

Portable forms are not authoritative.

They are entry points into the system and must:
- reference the evolving source  
- allow re-seeding as the system evolves  
- support inspection rather than bypass it  

Article-only loading may establish presentation cues.
Grounded operational use still depends on the seeder plus readable canonical
artifacts.

**Grounding behavior (expected):**
Portable contexts should:
- explicitly load the lens context  
- confirm what has been loaded  
- surface assumptions and uncertainties  

They bias behavior toward clarity, but do not guarantee compliance.

**Optional reflection:**
Portable contexts may include an explicit, inspectable mechanism
for reflecting insights back to the system (e.g. structured feedback
or proposed changes).

Reflection must remain:
- voluntary  
- visible  
- non-extractive  

It must not occur implicitly or without user awareness.

---

### 4. Continuity layer

**Role:**  
System orientation and memory.

**Function:**
- preserve direction over time  
- document evolution and decisions  
- prevent loss of intent  
- connect past, present, and future work  

This layer includes:
- architecture documents  
- vision anchors  
- roadmap reflections  
- system-level explanations  

**Continuity mechanism (initial):**
Continuity is maintained through:
- versioned documents  
- explicit change logs  
- preserved reasoning for major decisions  

No central authority governs this layer.  
Continuity emerges through documented trace over time.

---

## Relationship between layers

- **Seeds** provide depth  
- **Practice of Clarity** provides method  
- **Mandate Lenses** provide application  
- **Continuity Layer** provides stability over time  

None of these layers replace each other.  
They depend on each other without collapsing into one.

---

## Federation principle

The system is designed for federation without central control.

It operates through:
- shared seeds  
- independent mandate lenses  
- visible reasoning  

This enables:
- local adaptation without forced alignment  
- shared grounding without authority  
- evolution without requiring uniformity  

---

## Non-goals

This system is not:

- a centralized platform  
- a compliance framework  
- a fixed methodology  
- a belief system  
- a tool for control  

It does not:
- enforce agreement  
- remove conflict  
- guarantee correctness  

---

## Core constraint

The system only holds if:

**reasoning remains visible**

If reasoning becomes:
- hidden  
- compressed  
- abstracted beyond inspection  

the system drifts.

---

## Minimum requirement for reasoning visibility

Reasoning is considered visible when:

- assumptions are stated explicitly  
- uncertainty is acknowledged  
- inference steps are traceable  
- conclusions are distinguishable from inputs  

If these are not present, reasoning is insufficiently visible.

---

## Constraint: reasoning must be substantive

Reasoning is not considered visible if it is:

- purely declarative without supporting steps  
- reduced to generic disclaimers  
- disconnected from actual decisions  

Visible reasoning must meaningfully influence:
- interpretation  
- decisions  
- or outcomes  

Otherwise, it is considered performative.

Substantive reasoning cannot be determined mechanically.  
It must be evaluated through inspection of whether the reasoning
materially shapes the decision it accompanies.

## Current state

What exists:
- Seeds as root canon  
- Practice of Clarity as a public publication surface  
- Acts as the early public bridge  
- Continuity as a root package family  
- A first mandate lens package and context seeder  

What does not yet exist:
- validated mandate lenses in many real environments  
- community evolution of lenses  
- tested federation dynamics at scale  

---

## Constraint: first mandate lens

The first mandate lens must:

- contain no more than 5-7 defaults  
- be usable without additional explanation  
- be testable within a single real workflow  
- prioritize clarity over completeness  

---

## Creating a mandate lens

A mandate lens should:

- start from a real workflow  
- define a small number of defaults  
- expose reasoning for each default  
- be immediately usable without explanation  

A lens that cannot be used directly is not ready.

---

## Next step

The next step is not more architecture.

The next step is:

-> use the first real mandate lens  
-> keep it minimal and usable  
-> test it in practice  

This is where the system becomes real.

---

## Final note

This system is not meant to be finished.

It is meant to:
- remain inspectable  
- evolve without losing clarity  
- tolerate divergence without collapsing into control  

Clarity is not a state.  
It is a practice.
